In most courses, we had multiple exams throughout the semester, with each one covering a different topic or set of concepts. This meant that we had to stay on top of our studies at all times, and constantly review and practice in order to be prepared for each exam. But it wasn't just about exams - there was also a significant focus on project-based learning. In many of our courses, we were required to complete a major project as a significant portion of our grade. These projects were often complex and challenging, requiring us to apply the knowledge we had learned in class to real-world problems. While the workload was certainly demanding, I felt that the faculty at the University of Engineering and Computer Science were incredibly supportive and helpful. They were always available to answer questions, provide feedback on assignments, and offer guidance on our projects. Overall, my experience at the University of Engineering and Computer Science was a very positive one. It challenged me to work hard, learn new skills, and push myself to new heights.
